## Teams Assignments

### Attendance and Participation Assignment
Attendance and Participation count toward 30% of your final grade. To get 30 out of 30 in this category, you need to attend/participate in 30 course meetings. You can get up to 2 extra credit points in this category.
Here is how your grade for attendance and participation will be distributed:
- Unit 1: Attendance and Participation (Weeks 1-5)
- Unit 2: Attendance and Participation (Weeks 6-11)
- Unit 3: Attendance and Participation (Weeks 12-15)
- Unit 4: Attendance and Participation (Weeks 16-17)
Make sure your participation is reflected in our Class Exercises Notebook to get your points for attendance and participation.
Class Exercises Notebook: [Link]

### Quizzes Assignment
Quizzes are worth 10% of your final grade. To get a 10 out of 10 on quizzes, you'll need to make 3 deliveries:
- Unit 1 - Quiz Tracker (Weeks 2-4 with 3 total points available; Due at the end of Week 5)
- Unit 2 - Quiz Tracker (Weeks 6-10 with 5 total points available; Due at the end of Week 11)
- Unit 3 - Quiz Tracker (Weeks 12-14 with 3 total points available; Due at the end of Week 14)
You can also earn up to 1 extra credit point in this category:
- Extra Credit - Comprehensive Quiz Completion Screenshot (1 EC point available; Due at the end of Week 15)
A Quiz Tracker template for Unit 1 is attached. Fill the template out for Unit 1, and then continue adding to it and making deliveries that reflect your overall course progress for Units 2 and 3.

### Assignments Assignment
Assignments consisting of your original work and your peer evaluation of a classmate's work makes up 20% of your grade. Each assignment has 1 point allocated to your original work and 1 point allocated to your peer evaluation. To get 20 out of 20 in this category, be sure to work collaboratively on at least 10 assignments in the Class Assignments Notebook. If you complete all 11 available assignments, you can earn 2 extra credit points.
Unit 1 - Assignments (Weeks 2-4 with 6 total points available)
Unit 2 - Assignments (Weeks 6-10 with 10 total points available)
Unit 3 - Assignments (Weeks 12-14 with 10 total points available)

#### Final Project Presentation
About your delivery:
STEP 1
Follow these instructions to produce your final project: [https://alainamb.github.io/english-practice/templates/final-project-template.html?page=finalProject](https://eng301.locessentials.com/templates/final-project-template.html?page=finalProject)

STEP 2
After you've finished your project, prepare to have it peer reviewed.
Create a folder for your project in the Final Projects channel. 
- Name the folder: YourLastNames1-YourLastNames2_AbbreviatedProjectTitle
- Add all of the assets to your folder that your peers will need to access to complete their peer review
- Name each asset file following these conventions: YourLastNames-YourLastNames_AbbreviatedProjectComponent

STEP 3
Make a post to the Final Projects channel to announce that your content is ready for peer review.
Subject line: Project Title
Body of post:
- Authors: Let us know who contributed
- Short Description: A one sentence description of your project
- Why we should review your work: What specifically do we stand to learn that's useful?
- Anything else you'd like to share
- Link to your project folder

Your classmates will sign up to peer review your work as a comment upon your post.

Not following these delivery steps will result in point deductions from your grade.

#### Final Project Peer Evaluations
Follow these instructions when producing your final evaluations: https://eng301.locessentials.com/templates/final-project-template.html?page=peerEvaluation

Sign up for 3 peer reviews by commenting upon posts for the projects that interest you. Fill out one copy of the attached worksheet per project that you evaluate.
Name each copy: YourLastNames_PeerEval_Author1LastNames-Author2LastNames
Upload the three worksheets to this Teams assignment for 9 points.
Upload the three worksheets to each team's project folder in the Final Projects channel.
Comment on their post with a note that you've added your comments - or just with a high five!

Your peer evaluation grade:
Evaluation 1: 3 points
Evaluation 2: 3 points
Evaluation 3: 3 points
Administrative Work: 1 point (Files named correctly, Uploaded to folder, commented on post twice)
